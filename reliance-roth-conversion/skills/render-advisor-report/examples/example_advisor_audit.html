4 · Methodology

  1. Deterministic calculation. The engine is a pure function of (Scenario, TaxFactTable, engine_version). Same inputs → identical outputs every run. The run_id on the cover hashes the full input fingerprint.
  2. Versioned tax facts. Federal brackets, standard deductions, OBBBA senior bonus, IRMAA tiers, RMD divisors, LTCG stacking thresholds, and state rules load from a dated reference table (version 2025.11). Each fact carries a source citation in §5.
  3. Separation of math and narrative. Every number on every client one-pager is computed by the engine. Narrative prose is authored by the advisor and reviewed before client delivery. The LLM orchestrates the conversation; it does not compute tax numbers.
  4. Post-2028 projection. Federal brackets, standard deductions, and IRMAA tiers index forward at 2.5% / yr. The OBBBA senior bonus sunsets after 2028 and is zeroed thereafter.
  5. Conversion-tax funding. Tax on the conversion is paid from the taxable brokerage unless a budget override is specified.

6 · Known Limitations

  • Engine pays all conversion tax from taxable brokerage; insufficient brokerage produces a BROKERAGE_INSUFFICIENT halt.
  • IRMAA 2-year lookback uses a projected proxy for the first two projection years — flagged in run disclosures.
  • Post-2028 uses bracket_inflation drift; OBBBA senior bonus stays off; NIIT and SS thresholds stay static.
  • V1 filing statuses: MFJ and Single only. HOH and MFS refused at intake.
  • State facts outside the V1 seed list (TX, FL, AZ, AR, OK, MS) require onboarding before use.
