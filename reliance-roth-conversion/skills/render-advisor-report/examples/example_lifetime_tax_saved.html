Our Recommendation

Convert $750,000 from your Traditional IRA to a Roth IRA over the next five years.

Today you have a rare window — you’re past 65, retired, and not yet taking Required Minimum Distributions. We use these five years (2028–2032) to intentionally move taxable dollars into lower brackets, so the IRS doesn’t claim them at higher rates later.

Why This Matters

Without this plan, your Required Minimum Distributions at age 75 will push your income into the 12% brackets for the rest of your life, and higher still for the surviving spouse. This plan pays tax now at 22% so those same dollars never get taxed at higher rates later.

What It Costs

An additional ~$25,000 in federal tax this year, and a temporary increase in Medicare premiums of roughly $1,100/year for the years a conversion is reported. These costs repeat each conversion year in the schedule above.

What It Preserves

An estimated $39,000 in lifetime federal taxes, meaningfully smaller Required Minimum Distributions starting at age 75, and a larger Roth balance your heirs inherit without a 10-year forced-withdrawal tax bill.
