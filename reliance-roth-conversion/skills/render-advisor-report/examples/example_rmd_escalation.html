Our Recommendation

Convert $750,000 from your Traditional IRA to a Roth IRA over the next five years.

Your Required Minimum Distributions start at age 75. On a $1.2M IRA, they grow aggressively each year and push your income into progressively higher tax brackets. This plan reduces what the IRS forces you to withdraw later by moving those dollars now, at much lower rates.

Why This Matters

Without action, RMDs climb every year — and so does the bracket you’re taxed in. By age 80, “do nothing” pushes you into the 12% bracket. By age 85, the 12% bracket. This plan holds you at 22% or below for life by pre-paying tax on terms you choose.

What It Costs

An additional ~$25,000 in federal tax this year, and a temporary increase in Medicare premiums of roughly $1,100/year for the years a conversion is reported.

What It Preserves

A lifetime ceiling at the 22% bracket instead of climbing into 12%+, meaningfully smaller Required Minimum Distributions from age 75 onward, and a larger Roth balance your heirs inherit tax-free.
